Chemical tests

Get an adult to make up the household soda solution, as it is highly caustic. When making up the solutions wear rubber gloves and take care not to get the solutions on your skin or clothes.

  • Bleach. Put a small amount of the thin bleach (not the thick bleach which has other chemicals in) in a small labelled dropper bottle (old eye-dropper bottles are ideal) .

  • Caustic soda. Measure 100ml of water ( tap water will do) and add quarter teaspoon of household caustic soda ( use cook measuring set for quarter of a teaspoon). The solution will heat up and when it has cooled put it in a separate labelled dropper bottle *** health and safety warning.

  • To do the tests: Scratch the surface of the lichen carefully to remove the upper layer (cortex) and expose the fungal layer (medulla) beneath. Note that you do not need to do this on thin crusts)

  • Put a drop of either bleach (C) or dilute caustic soda (K) on the scratched spot and watch for a colour change.

  • Bleach response: fast reaction turning pink or red if positive but fading rapidly, signified by □ in the text .

  • Caustic soda response: slower reaction (c.1min) and if positive may turn yellow, orange or blood red signified by □ in the text.
